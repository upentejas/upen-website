Why this strategy

🎯

Structural mean-reversion

SGX aromatics spreads are anchored by fundamental supply-demand economics. When they deviate from fair value — driven by cracker outages, inventory builds, or seasonal demand shifts — they reliably revert. Our model identifies and trades these windows systematically.

🔗

Uncorrelated to equities & macro

Spread returns are driven by petrochemical sector fundamentals, not interest rates, equity risk premium, or general market sentiment. This makes the strategy a genuine diversifier in a multi-asset portfolio — returns that behave independently of traditional asset classes.

🏭

Physical market information edge

Venkatesh Raman's network across Singapore, Korea, Japan, and China's petrochemical complex provides early visibility into supply disruptions, cracker maintenance schedules, and cargo flow shifts — intelligence that is not reflected in public data and that sharpens entry and exit timing.

Risk management

🛡️

Active hedge management

Every open position carries a predefined stop-loss and is paired with dynamic hedges. Drawdown is actively managed throughout the position lifecycle — not just at entry.

📐

Rules-based, no discretion

Entry and exit signals are fully model-defined. No overrides, no gut-feel adjustments. The strategy's discipline is the edge — it doesn't deviate in volatile markets.

🌐

Zero crude oil directional exposure

All positions are spread trades between two aromatics products. Crude oil price movements affect both legs similarly, meaning the strategy is structurally insulated from crude volatility.

📊

Position sizing discipline

Position sizes are calibrated to model confidence and spread volatility — not fixed. Higher-conviction, lower-volatility setups receive larger allocations. No averaging down
